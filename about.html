Guiding Fellow People With Learning Disabilities

I have a form of autism known as "Asperger's Syndrome". You might hear people say it means that someone is "wired differently", and I agree with that. But, when people ask me what that means, I say "I'm built different". Not to start a sentimental pity party, but growing up autistic was really strange and really tough, it comes with its own set of unique challenges. Don't even get me started on being an autistic college student in a pandemic lol. But, I love the person I turned into over the years, being "wired differently" allowed me to become "built different". It's why I have the mentality and foundation to become an excellent programmer and agilist. My school (The University of Rhode Island) gave me an opportunity to become a role model and guide to other students who have autism and other learning disabilities. I help other people achieve their full potential in life, and that drives me to be the best I can be everyday. Currently, I work with a program in Rhode Island called the Office of Rehabilitation Services to speak with incoming college students with disabilities, as well as their parents, to guide them in their journeys of higher education.
